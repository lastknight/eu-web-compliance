## Area G · Forms, data collection and consent

**Rationale**: forms are the points where the site actively collects personal data supplied by the data subject: contact, registration, newsletter sign-up, quote request, restricted area, checkout. Every form is a processing operation and must be assessed on four levels. First, the quality of consent when this is the legal basis: it must be freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous, therefore collected through a positive act (no pre-ticked boxes), separated by purpose and not bundled with acceptance of the terms. Second, minimisation: every mandatory field must be justified by the stated purpose, and the defaults must be the most protective ones. Third, transparency at the point of collection: the privacy notice (or a link to it) must be present on the form, before submission. Fourth, the destination of the data: what the user types must travel only to the controller, without third-party scripts (session replay, key logging) intercepting it even before submission. Downstream of collection, two obligations close the loop: the ability to withdraw as easily as consent was given (one-click unsubscribe in marketing emails) and the documentable recording of the consent itself, because the burden of proof lies with the controller. On e-commerce sites a fifth level is added: the legal basis for the account, because forcing registration in order to buy, when the purchase is possible as a guest, is often a processing operation without a valid basis. The technical scan maps the forms and observes the flow of data; judging the quality of consent and minimisation often requires reading the texts and cross-referencing with the intake.

**Legal bases**: Art. 4(11) GDPR (definition of consent: freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous) [N1], Art. 6 GDPR (legal bases for processing) [N1], Art. 7 GDPR (conditions for consent: demonstrability, withdrawal, non-conditionality) [N1], Art. 5(1)(c) GDPR (minimisation) [N1], Art. 5(1)(a) GDPR (lawfulness, fairness, transparency) [N1], Art. 5(1)(e) GDPR (storage limitation) [N1], Art. 5(2) GDPR (accountability) [N1], Art. 8 GDPR (children's consent, with the threshold lowered to 14 years in Italy by Art. 2-quinquies of the Codice Privacy, the Italian Privacy Code) [N1, N2], Art. 9 GDPR (special categories of data and explicit consent) [N1], Art. 11 GDPR (processing not requiring identification) [N1], Art. 12/13 GDPR (information at the time of collection) [N1], Art. 14 GDPR (information for data not collected from the data subject) [N1], Art. 21(2)-(3) GDPR (objection to direct marketing) [N1], Art. 25 GDPR (privacy by design and by default) [N1], Art. 32 GDPR (security of processing) [N1], Art. 130 of the Codice Privacy (unsolicited communications and soft spam under paragraph 4) [N2], Art. 5(3) of Directive 2002/58/EC ePrivacy (for unsubscribe and trackers on forms) [N3], CJEU case C-673/17 Planet49 (no pre-ticked box) [J1], CJEU case C-252/21 Meta v. **Engagement note**: checks G-06, G-10 and G-11 involve a test sign-up or submission with addresses and synthetic data controlled by the auditor: they must be carried out only with the client's authorisation for the assessment and using disposable identifiers, never real third-party data. Check G-13 requires only navigation of the checkout, without completing the purchase or entering real payment data. Checks G-09, G-12 and G-14 are documentary and not verifiable from the site side alone: the scan produces the technical evidence (forms, fields, flows, presence or absence of guest mode), the intake provides the organisational part (age verification, consent log, origin of the lists and information under Art. 14). The strongest finding arises from the gap between what the forms collect and what the client documents as being managed.

**Client questions (Area G)**

**Newsletter and lists**
- Do you have a newsletter? With which platform (Mailchimp, Kit, Brevo, etc.)? Do you use double opt-in? How was the historical list built and do you have verifiable opt-in for the subscribers already present?
- Do your lists contain contacts purchased or enriched from third parties? How have you fulfilled the information obligation under Art. 14 towards these people and how can they object?

**Lead generation and B2B prospecting**
- Do you do lead generation? Through which channels (site forms, landing pages, Meta/LinkedIn lead ads, purchase or enrichment of lists)?
- Do you do B2B prospecting towards professionals? On what basis do you found it (consent or legitimate interest) and is the solicitation relevant to the recipient's role?

**CRM and proof of consent**
- Do you have an active CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, etc.)? Does the data collected from the site forms flow into it automatically and with what mapping of consents?
- Where and how do you record the proof of consent (date, time, method, purpose, version of the privacy notice)? Would you be able to reconstruct the consent of a single subscriber if challenged?
- Is marketing consent separated from the other consents in your forms, or is it grouped with acceptance of the terms or with registration?

**Email marketing and form tracking**
- Do your marketing emails contain a one-click unsubscribe link and the List-Unsubscribe header? How quickly do you process unsubscribes?
- Do you use session recording, replay or heatmap tools (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity, FullStory, etc.)? Are they configured to exclude or mask the form fields?

**Minors, special data and e-commerce**
- Does your audience include minors under 14 years of age? If so, how do you verify age and how do you collect the consent of the holder of parental responsibility?
- Does any form collect data that falls within the special categories (health, orientation, beliefs, biometric data)? On what legal basis and with what safeguards?
- Do you sell online (e-commerce)? Does the checkout allow purchase as a guest or do you impose the creation of an account to buy? If the account is mandatory, on what legal basis do you found it?
