## Area M · AI features on the site (GDPR + AI Act)

**Applies if**: entirely conditional area. It applies if the site integrates at least one of: a chatbot or conversational assistant, LLM-based functions (generative search, summarization, drafting), generation or manipulation of content (text, images, audio, video), or AI-supported profiling or automated decisions. If none of these are present, the area does not apply and must be marked as not in scope in the report.

**Rationale**: when a site exposes AI functions, two distinct bodies of law stack up. The GDPR governs the processing of personal data flowing into and out of the model (user input, generated output, use of prompts for training): the positions of the EDPB ChatGPT Taskforce clarify that technical impossibility does not justify non-compliance and that the burden of proof falls on the controller [S43]. The AI Act adds specific transparency obligations (Art. 50) and a risk classification with a core of prohibited practices (Art. 5) [S42, S92]. Many general GDPR checks are already covered by Areas A-J: here we add only what is AI-specific, citing the existing IDs where the topic is already handled elsewhere (in particular B-03, B-10, G-08, H-01, H-05).

**Legal bases**: Reg. EU 2016/679 (GDPR), in particular Art. 5, Art. 6, Art. 9, Art. 13, Art. 16, Art. 17, Art. 22, Art. 28, Art. 35 [S43]; Reg. EU 2024/1689 (AI Act), in particular Art. 5 (prohibited practices), Art. 6 and Art. 50 (transparency), Art. 113 (start of application) [S42, S92]. Note on deadlines: the transparency obligations of Art. 50 AI Act apply from 2 August 2026; the prohibition of the practices in Art. 5 has already been in force since 2 February 2025.

**Client questions (Area M)**

**AI scope and functions**
- Does the site integrate a chatbot, a conversational assistant or LLM-based functions (generative search, summarization, drafting)? With which provider or model (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, self-hosted solution)?
- Does the site generate or manipulate synthetic content (text, images, audio, video) shown to the public? In which sections?
- Do you publish AI-generated text on matters of public interest (news, in-depth pieces)? With what human and editorial review process?

**Risk classification and prohibited practices**
- For what purpose is the AI system used and with what persuasive mechanisms? (needed to rule out practices prohibited under Art. 5: manipulation, exploitation of vulnerabilities, social scoring, emotion recognition)
- Have you classified the system's risk according to the AI Act and produced a classification memo?

**Legal bases and training**
- On what legal basis do you process prompt inputs, generated outputs and any use of prompts for training? Are the phases distinct in your analysis?
- Are user inputs used to train or improve the model? Is an opt-out provided? How do you inform the user?
- Does the privacy policy explicitly describe AI processing, the model's limits and the use for training?

**Rights and output accuracy**
- How do you handle rectification and erasure requests referring to the model's outputs?
- How do you measure and correct output accuracy, beyond the disclaimers?

**Impact assessment and providers**
- Does the system perform profiling or automated decisions with legal or significant effects on individuals? Have you carried out a DPIA and involved the DPO?
- Do the contracts with the AI providers define the provider/deployer roles, the Art. 50 transparency obligations and, where they process data on your behalf, a DPA under Art. 28?
