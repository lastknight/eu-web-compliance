## Area O · Beyond the GDPR: DSA, DMA, Digital Fairness Act

**Applies if**: the entire area is CONDITIONAL. Do not touch a corporate site that carries only its own content: it is out of scope for the DSA. The area applies at an increasing scale based on an applicability gate:
- **DSA** applies only if the site **hosts third-party content** (comments, reviews, UGC, forums, uploads) or is an **online platform / marketplace**. A brochure site that publishes only its own content stays out of scope.
- **DMA** does not create obligations for a normal site: it creates **rights** towards gatekeepers for anyone who is a business user (whoever sells on Amazon, develops apps, manages pages/accounts, invests in Meta/Google Ads). To be presented as an opportunity and an awareness, not as compliance.
- **DFA** (Digital Fairness Act) is a proposal under consultation, not yet in force: a forward-looking radar, never a current obligation.

All the checks in this area are conditional or informative. The `[COND: ...]` tag at the head of the check indicates the context that triggers it.

**Rationale**: the GDPR is not the only regulation that touches the web surface. The DSA (EU Regulation 2022/2065, applicable from 17 February 2024) imposes increasing obligations on those who host third-party content, with sanction exposure of up to 6 percent of annual worldwide turnover. The DMA (EU Regulation 2022/1925, applicable from 2 May 2023) reverses the perspective: for a normal site it is not a burden but a lever, because it assigns rights towards the large platforms. The DFA is the front that is coming: the Commission closed the consultation on 24 October 2025 and is preparing the legislative proposal on dark patterns, addictive design, deceptive personalization and price marketing. Mapping today where you are exposed avoids chasing it tomorrow.

**Legal bases**: Regulation (EU) 2022/2065 (DSA), Arts. 11, 12, 13, 14, 16, 17, 20, 22, 25, 26, 27, 28, 30, 31, 32; micro and small enterprise exemption Art. 19; in Italy AGCOM (the Italian communications authority) and the Digital Services Coordinator [S60, S61]. Regulation (EU) 2022/1925 (DMA), Art. 6(10) and prohibitions on anti-steering and data combination [S62, S63]. Digital Fairness Act, proposal under consultation (17 July 2025, 24 October 2025), current floor in Directive 2005/29/EC (unfair commercial practices) and Directive 2011/83/EU (consumer rights) [S64, S65].

**Client questions (Area O)**

**Applicability gate**
- Does the site host user-generated content (comments, reviews, forums, uploads, public profiles)? If not, the DSA is out of scope and the area stops here.
- Is the service an online platform (putting users in contact with each other or allowing publication by third parties) or a marketplace (allowing distance contracts between consumers and third-party sellers)?
- What is the size of the enterprise (number of employees and turnover)? Qualifying as a micro or small enterprise exempts it from the obligations of Arts. 20-28 DSA.
- Is the provider established in the EU? If not, the legal representative under Art. 13 is needed.

**DSA moderation and transparency**
- Who handles content moderation and with which tools, including algorithmic ones? Is there a channel for reporting illegal content?
- When you remove or restrict content, does the user receive a statement of reasons and information about remedies?
- Is the advertising shown labeled as such? Do you use recommender systems or algorithmic ranking of content?
- Does the audience include minors? Do you run profiled advertising on them?

**Rights towards gatekeepers (DMA)**
- Do you sell on third-party marketplaces, publish apps on stores or manage pages/accounts on large platforms? Do you invest in advertising on Meta or Google?
- Are you already extracting your transactional data via the gatekeepers' APIs (access provided by Art. 6(10) DMA)? If not, it is an unexploited right.

**Digital Fairness Act radar**
- Does the site sell, sign people up or personalize offers and prices towards consumers? Is unsubscribing from a subscription or a newsletter as simple as subscribing?
- Do you engage influencers or affiliates? Is sponsored content labeled as commercial?
