## Area N · Voice channels and automated calls

**Applies if**: this entire area applies if the client conducts outbound telephone prospecting, uses conversational voice bots or IVRs, or integrates virtual voice assistants. If the client does none of this, the area is out of scope.

**Rationale**: the automated voice channel combines three disciplines that coexist and must be respected together: the GDPR (legal basis, rights, security), the Italian rules on unsolicited communications (Art. 130 Codice Privacy, the Italian Privacy Code, and the Registro Pubblico delle Opposizioni, the Italian public opt-out register), and the AI Act when the speaker is an automated system [S93]. Compared to areas A-J, here the processing does not go through a website but through a phone call: consent is prior and specific to the automated tool, transparency plays out in the first seconds of the call, and the voice itself can become biometric data. The most frequent violations found by the authorities are, in order: absence of valid consent for B2C prospecting, opt-out not respected or not extended to all channels, failure to check the opt-out register, excessive retention of recordings [S93].

**Legal bases**: Art. 130 para. 1 Codice Privacy (Italian Privacy Code, D.lgs. 196/2003), consent for automated calling systems without an operator [N2]; Registro Pubblico delle Opposizioni (Italian public opt-out register, Law of 11 January 2018 no. 5 and Presidential Decree 149/2018); Arts. 6, 7, 9, 13, 21, 22, 25, 35 GDPR [N1]; AI Act Art. 50, Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, transparency of systems that interact with natural persons [S93]; EDPB Guidelines 02/2021 on virtual voice assistants [S45]; CNIL (the French DPA) practice on prospecting via automated calling systems and on the retention of recordings [S44, S93].

**Client questions (Area N)**

**Prospecting and consent**
- Do you make outbound calls for commercial or prospecting purposes? Toward consumers (B2C), professionals (B2B) or both?
- Are the calls fully automated (system without operator, voice bot, conversational IVR) or is there always a human operator?
- How was the consent of the numbers you call collected? Where do you retain the proof, for each contact?
- Do you consult the Registro Pubblico delle Opposizioni before each campaign? How frequently do you redo the filtering? Do you treat registration in the RPO as a withdrawal of previous consents?

**Transparency and call recording**
- Does the bot declare that it is an automated system at the start of the call? With what formula and in which seconds? Is the announcement repeated at each callback?
- Do you record the calls? For what purposes, with what prior notice and for how long do you retain audio and transcripts? Who can re-listen to them?
- Do you use the recordings to train or improve the model, with human review or labelling? On what legal basis?

**Opt-out and data subject rights**
- How do you handle a refusal expressed during the call? Does the bot recognize the opt-out by voice and by key? Is the confirmation immediate? Does the opt-out propagate to all campaigns and to a centralized suppression list?

**Voice biometrics and automated decisions**
- Do you use the voice to identify or authenticate the person (voiceprint)? Where are the voice models generated and stored? Do you offer a non-biometric alternative?
- Does the bot assign scores or make decisions that affect the person (scoring, application rejection, credit, insurance)? Is there human intervention and the possibility to contest?

**DPIA, hosting and providers**
- Have you carried out a DPIA for the voice system?
- Where are audio, transcripts and the CRM hosted? Have you signed the DPA with the voice bot provider?
