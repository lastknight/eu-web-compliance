## Area K · CRM, newsletter and direct marketing

**Applies if**: entire area conditional. It applies if the client does email marketing or newsletters, does lead generation, or uses a CRM to manage contacts, customers or prospects. If none of these conditions apply, the area is out of scope.

**Rationale**: consent and rights do not end at the website. The weak point of direct marketing is the data lifecycle beyond the form: consent collected on the site must reach the CRM together with its proof, the legal regime changes between B2C and B2B, the opt-out must propagate to all systems and not only to the list it came from, and lists must be kept clean with a defined retention. Violations here are not visible by crawling the homepage: they emerge from intake, from CRM exports and from unsubscribe tests. This area extends onto the back office side the checks already present on the site: proof of consent (C-10), double opt-in and soft spam on the form (G-06, G-07), one-click unsubscribe (H-03), recipients in policy (B-05), data from third parties (B-11).

**Legal bases**: Art. 6 and Art. 7 GDPR (legal bases and conditions for consent), Art. 21 (objection to direct marketing), Art. 14 (data collected from third parties), Art. 5(1)(d)(e) (accuracy and storage limitation), Art. 5(2) (accountability), Art. 32 and Art. 25 (security and protection by default); Art. 130 and Art. 130(4) of the Codice Privacy (Italian Privacy Code, D.lgs. 196/2003, unsolicited communications and soft spam); Garante (the Italian DPA) spam guidelines of 4 July 2013. Sources: [S40], [S41], [S44], [S90], [S91], [S94].

**Client questions (Area K)**

**Tools and provenance of the lists**
- Do you do email marketing, newsletters, lead generation or use a CRM? With which tools (CRM and sending platform)?
- How was the contact list built: direct subscriptions, soft spam on existing customers, purchased lists, collection from web or social?

**Consent and legal bases**
- Does the consent collected on the site flow into the CRM together with its proof (date, time, method, version of the notice)?
- Do you distinguish B2C contacts (consent) from B2B ones (legitimate interest)? For B2B, do you have a documented balancing test (LIA)?

**Opt-out and retention**
- When a contact unsubscribes, does the opt-out propagate to all connected systems? Do you maintain a suppression list that resists reimports?
- What retention do you apply to leads, prospects and terminated customers? Who deletes or anonymizes inactive contacts, and after how long?

**Sharing with partners and CRM access**
- Do you share data with commercial partners? On what legal basis and with what consent collected?
- Who has access to the CRM and with what permissions? Is there a differentiated access profile by role with access logging?
