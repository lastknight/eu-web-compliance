## Area E · Third parties, non-EU transfers and supply chain

**Rationale**: every third-party script, pixel, font or iframe loaded by the site is a recipient of personal data: it receives at least the IP address and browsing metadata, often much more. The complete inventory of third parties is the map of the site's actual processing operations, to be compared with the privacy notice (Area B) and with the record of processing activities. Every host contacted may entail a communication of data to a third country, so it must be geolocated and traced back to a valid transfer mechanism under Chapter V. Compliance does not end on the technical level: behind every third party there must be a processor appointed under an Art. 28 contract, its sub-processors must be known and authorised, the supplier must have been assessed before onboarding and, if outside the EEA, the transfer must rest on a documented safeguard with a Transfer Impact Assessment. The technical scan detects the facts (who is contacted); the documentary checks verify that a legitimate contractual chain corresponds to those facts.

**Legal bases**: Arts. 44-49 GDPR (Chapter V, transfers to third countries) [N1], Art. 45 (adequacy decision) [N1], Art. 46 (appropriate safeguards, including SCCs) [N1], Art. 47 (binding corporate rules, BCR) [N1], Art. 28 GDPR (processor, contract, sub-processors, audit) [N1], Art. 13(1)(e)-(f) GDPR (information on recipients and transfers) [N1], Art. 30(1)(d) GDPR (record of recipients) [N1], Art. 5(2) GDPR (accountability) [N1], Art. 5(3) Directive 2002/58/EC and Art. 122 Codice Privacy (Italian Privacy Code, D.lgs. 196/2003) (storage/access to terminal via embeds and tags) [N3, N2], Art. 32 GDPR (security of the technical supply chain) [N1], Schrems II judgment C-311/18 (invalidation of Privacy Shield, obligation to assess the transfer context) [J2], EU-US Data Privacy Framework adequacy decision, July 2023 [N4], Garante (the Italian DPA) decision on Google Analytics, June 2022 [G2], Google Fonts case, LG München I, 3 O 17493/20, January 2022 [J3]. Methodological and classification support: EDPB Website Auditing Tool [S4, S6], EDPS Website Evidence Collector [S7], Blacklight [S8]; the breadth of obligations on vendors, DPAs, transfers, advertising pixels and web agencies drawn from authority and vendor checklists [S1, S2, S9, S10, S11, S95, S51].

**Engagement note**: checks E-11, E-12, E-13 and E-14 are documentary and not verifiable from the site side alone. The technical scan (E-01..E-10, E-15 for the detection part) produces the factual list of third parties and transfers; these four checks compare that list with the contractual chain declared by the client. The value of the finding lies precisely in the gap between what the browser observes and what the client documents.

**Client questions (Area E)**

**Processors and DPAs**
- Do you have an up-to-date list of the suppliers that process personal data on your behalf, with a signed DPA (Art. 28) for each? Who maintains it? (the typical processors of a site: Google Analytics, the CMS/hosting, the CDN, the newsletter platform, the CRM, error tracking such as Sentry, the payment gateway, the captcha)
- Is the site developed or managed by an external web agency? If the agency accesses the backoffice, receives form data or provides your hosting, have you appointed it as an external processor?
- For each primary supplier, do you have the list of sub-processors and are you informed of and have you authorised their use?
- Do you have a supplier assessment procedure before activation (review of privacy policy and security measures) and an audit plan for high-risk vendors?

**Non-EEA transfers and data localisation**
- For suppliers outside the EEA: which transfer mechanism do you use (adequacy, DPF for the US, SCCs, BCR)? For SCCs, do you have a documented Transfer Impact Assessment per country?
- Do you know in which countries the data physically resides (hosting, backup, CDN, CRM and newsletter cloud, payment provider)?

**Newsletter, CRM and lead generation**
- Do you have a newsletter? With which platform (Mailchimp, Kit, Brevo, etc.) and where is the processing based?
- Do you do lead generation or have active CRMs (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.) that automatically receive the data collected on the site? Where are they hosted?

**Tag manager and advertising pixels**
- Who has access to the tag manager? Is there an approval process before adding a new tag or tracker, and who verifies that the loaded tags correspond to those authorised?
- Do you use advertising or remarketing pixels (Facebook/Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, Google Ads)? Do they fire only after consent? Have you declared them in the privacy notice and cookie policy with opt-out instructions?

**reCAPTCHA and remote fonts**
- Do you use Google reCAPTCHA on forms or login? Have you assessed alternatives that do not transfer to the US?
- Are the site's fonts served from remote Google Fonts or self-hosted?
