## Area H · Data subject rights and channels for exercising them

**Rationale**: the data subject must be able to exercise their rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) with an ease comparable to that with which they provided their data. The audit checks two distinct planes: the existence and functionality of the declared channels (dedicated email, form, account self-service areas) and compliance with the deadlines and process guarantees (response time, identity verification, notification to recipients, register of requests). Actual responsiveness can only be measured with an agreed live test: everything else is verifiable on the site and in the account area.

**Legal bases**: Art. 12 GDPR (facilitated exercise of rights, concise and accessible form, response deadlines, identity verification), Arts. 15-22 GDPR (the substantive rights: access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection), Art. 13(2)(b)-(d) and Art. 7(3) GDPR (information about the existence of the rights, the right to lodge a complaint and the right to withdraw consent), Art. 19 GDPR (notification to recipients), Art. 38(4) and Art. 37(7) GDPR (contact details and reachability of the DPO), Art. 21(3) GDPR and Art. 130 Codice Privacy (Italian Privacy Code, D.lgs. 196/2003) (objection to direct marketing and opt-out), Art. 5(1)(f) and Art. 5(2) GDPR (integrity and confidentiality, accountability) [N1, N2]; Garante (the Italian DPA) spam guidelines of 4 July 2013 [G3].

**Client questions (Area H)**

**Management and process of requests**
- Who receives and handles data subject requests (access, erasure, rectification, objection)? Is there a written procedure with an SLA and a register of requests?
- How many requests have you received in the last year and, on average, how long did you take to respond? Have you ever used the two-month extension?
- How do you verify the identity of a person submitting a request before handing over the data? What additional information do you ask for?

**Data extraction and export**
- Are you able to extract all of a person's data from all systems (site, CRM, newsletter, e-commerce, management software) and deliver it in a commonly used electronic format?
- For portability, can you provide the data in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV)? Is there an export function or endpoint?

**DPO and marketing channels**
- Do you have an appointed DPO? Are their contact details published in the notice and is it a genuinely monitored channel?
- Do you have a newsletter and marketing communications? On which platform? Does every send contain a one-click unsubscribe link and the List-Unsubscribe header? Do you do lead generation or campaigns with custom audiences that feed direct marketing?

**Account areas and notice**
- Does the service provide account areas for users? If so, can the user modify their profile, export their data and delete their account on their own?
- Does the notice list all the data subject's rights, including the right to withdraw consent at any time and the right to lodge a complaint with the Garante?

**Notification to third parties and restriction**
- When you erase, rectify or restrict a person's data, do you notify the action to the third-party recipients to whom the data had been transmitted (external CRMs, suppliers, partners)? With which procedure?
- Do you have a mechanism to freeze the processing (restriction) of an account or a record without erasing it?
