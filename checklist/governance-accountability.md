## Area I · Governance, accountability, and the data lifecycle

**Rationale**: the website is the visible surface, but compliance rests on an organizational framework that a technical scan sees only indirectly. This area covers two planes. The first is intake: defining the real perimeter (how many domains, which channels, e-commerce, apps, advertising) before crawling, because it determines the scope and severity of every finding. The second is the backbone of accountability under Art. 5(2): the controller must not only be compliant, it must be able to demonstrate compliance with living, up-to-date documents. Record of processing activities, appointment of the DPO and of the EU representative, DPIA, privacy by design, legitimate interest assessment, contracts with processors, breach management procedure, retention plan with automatic deletion, staff training, and periodic review. These are largely controls that are not observable from the front end: they are verified on a documentary basis with the client and cross-checked against the technical evidence gathered in the other areas (third-party inventory in Area E, cookie durations in Area C, form minimization in Area G, policy dates in Area A).

**Legal bases**: Art. 5(2) GDPR (accountability, the burden of demonstrating compliance), Art. 24 GDPR (responsibility of the controller), Art. 30 GDPR (records of processing activities), Arts. 37-39 GDPR (designation, position, and tasks of the DPO, including staff awareness and training), Art. 27 GDPR (representative of a controller not established in the Union), Art. 35 GDPR (data protection impact assessment), Art. 25 GDPR (data protection by design and by default), Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest and its balancing), Art. 28 GDPR (contract with the processor), Arts. 33-34 GDPR (notification and communication of breaches), Art. 5(1)(e) GDPR (storage limitation) and Art. 17 GDPR (erasure), Art. 32 GDPR (organizational measures and training as part of security) [N1]; for the e-commerce and app perimeter, Art. 122 and Art. 130 of the Codice Privacy (Italian Privacy Code, D.lgs. 196/2003) also apply [N2].

**Client questions (Area I)**

**Perimeter and processing channels**
- How many sites and domains do you manage? Do they share the same infrastructure and the same policies, or does each have its own? (defines the crawling perimeter and which findings apply to which domains)
- Do you have an e-commerce? Do you handle payments directly or through a PSP (Stripe, PayPal, Nexi, etc.)? Do you store payment data and, if so, where?
- Are there mobile apps connected to the site or the service? With which third-party SDKs (analytics, advertising, crash reporting)?
- Do you do advertising (Google Ads, Meta, TikTok, LinkedIn)? Do you use custom audiences or upload contact lists to the platforms?
- Does the site have B2B or B2C restricted areas with third-party data (clients' clients, employees, suppliers)?

**Record, roles, and mandatory figures**
- Do you have records of processing activities (Art. 30)? Are they built on a data mapping and do they contain all the elements of Art. 30? Who maintains them, how often do you update them, and can we receive an extract to compare it with what we detect technically?
- Who is the internal referent for privacy and accountability? How are reporting lines and responsibilities defined? Have you appointed a DPO, internal or external? Are they independent, free of conflicts of interest, and reporting to top management? Are their contact details published in the notice and is it a genuinely monitored channel?
- Is the controller established outside the EU? If so, have you designated a representative in the Union (Art. 27) and do you indicate their contact?

**Preliminary assessments and privacy by design**
- Have you carried out a DPIA for high-risk processing (large-scale profiling, monitoring, special categories)? With which methodology (WP29, CNIL)? Who conducted it, when was it reviewed, and where is the documentation kept?
- For processing based on legitimate interest, have you carried out and documented a legitimate interest assessment (LIA) before starting?
- Do you apply privacy by design and by default criteria (opt-out by default for marketing, more protective settings preselected, minimization of the fields collected)? Who validates new forms and new processing operations before release? Does the principle also weigh in the choice of new suppliers and tools?

**Processors, breaches, and retention**
- Do you have an Art. 28 contract (DPA) with every supplier that processes data on your behalf (hosting, email, analytics, CRM, PSP)? Do you carry out due diligence on their security measures and periodically verify their compliance?
- Do you have a breach management procedure (detection, assessment, notification to the Garante within 72 hours, communication to data subjects) and do you keep a breach register under Art. 33(5)?
- Is there a data retention plan by category? Does deletion or anonymization at expiry happen automatically (scheduled jobs, TTL) or manually? How is data destroyed at end of life? Do the periods declared in the notice match what you actually do?

**Training and periodic review**
- Do staff with access to data receive periodic data protection training (entry and refresher)? Are there materials, participation records, and learning checks? Do critical roles receive advanced training?
- At what cadence do you review policies, records of processing activities, and the retention plan? Who oversees this review and what triggers it (fixed deadline, new processing, change of suppliers or of destination countries)? Do you have an internal or external audit program on compliance?
