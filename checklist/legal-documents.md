## Area A · Legal documents: presence and reachability

**Rationale**: the privacy notice is the first transparency obligation. If it is missing, unreachable, unreadable or outdated, the entire rest of the audit starts out already compromised. Transparency requires a concise, intelligible and easily accessible form: it is the gateway to every other obligation and the first element that a supervisory authority or a data subject checks. This area verifies the physical existence and findability of the documents, not their content (which is the subject of Area B).

**Legal bases**: Art. 12(1) GDPR (information in a concise, transparent, intelligible and easily accessible form) [N1], Art. 13 GDPR (privacy notice for data collected from the data subject) [N1], Art. 14 GDPR (indirect collection) [N1], Art. 5(2) GDPR (accountability: the controller must be able to demonstrate compliance, including the history of privacy notices) [N1]. For the cookie policy: Art. 122 of the Codice Privacy (Italian Privacy Code, D.lgs. 196/2003) [N2], Art. 5(3) of Directive 2002/58/EC [N3], Garante (the Italian DPA) cookie guidelines of 10 June 2021, doc-web 9677876 [G1].

**Client questions (Area A)**

**Drafting and version history**
- Who drafted the notices (privacy and cookie) and when were they last updated? Is there a periodic review process?
- Do you keep a history of previous versions of the policies, with the effective dates of each? In what form (repository, CMS with versioning, document archive)?

**Languages and publication format**
- In how many languages is the site available and in how many are the notices? Who handles the translation of the policies?
- Are the notices served as HTML pages of the site or as downloadable PDF files/documents?

**Consistency across channels and notification of changes**
- Are there versions of the policies for other channels (mobile app, physical points of sale, telephone contact, campaign landing page) that need to be kept consistent with those of the site?
- When you modify a policy substantially, do you notify users of the change? Through which channel (email, banner, on-page notice)? (link to B-14)
