## Area J · Data breaches: preparedness and notification

**Rationale**: a personal data breach is not a merely possible eventuality but an event to be planned for, and the GDPR does not sanction the breach in itself: it sanctions the lack of preparedness. The controller must be able to realise that a breach has occurred, to contain it, to notify it to the supervisory authority within a strict deadline, to communicate it to data subjects when the risk is high, to document every incident and to learn from it. It is an almost entirely back-end and organisational area: a web scan captures only external indicators of it (a channel for receiving security reports, the reachability of the DPO contact that must appear in the notification), while its core (detection, response plan, notification procedure, breach register, forensic capability) is verified through documentary review at intake. The temporal pivot is Art. 33: the 72-hour countdown starts from when the controller "becomes aware" of the breach, so without a detection capability the entire obligation is structurally impossible to fulfil. For this reason the area must be read together with Area F (security, which reduces probability and impact: encryption under Art. 34(3)(a), logging under F-14, pseudonymisation) and with Area I (Art. 30 register and governance): breach preparedness is the practical proof of accountability. The ICO in its Accountability Framework isolates a specific control area, "breach response and monitoring", which requires procedures to detect, manage, record, assess, notify, communicate and review breaches: the same chain set out here [S48, N69, N70, N71, N72].

**Legal bases**: Art. 33 GDPR (notification of the breach to the supervisory authority), Art. 33(1) (without undue delay, where feasible within 72 hours, unless a risk to the rights and freedoms is unlikely), Art. 33(2) (the processor informs the controller without undue delay), Art. 33(3) (content of the notification), Art. 33(4) (information that may be provided in phases), Art. 33(5) (documentation of any breach regardless of notification), Art. 34 GDPR (communication of the breach to the data subject), Art. 34(1) (obligation in case of high risk), Art. 34(2) (content of the communication in clear language), Art. 34(3) (exemptions: data rendered unintelligible e.g. encryption, subsequent measures that avert the risk, disproportionate effort with alternative public communication), Art. 32(1)(b) (confidentiality, integrity, availability), Art. 32(1)(c) (ability to restore availability and access in the event of an incident), Art. 32(1)(d) (periodic testing and assessment of the effectiveness of the measures), Art. 24(1) (measures reviewed and updated), Art. 5(2) (accountability), Recitals 85-88 GDPR [N1]. At national level: Art. 2-bis and notification obligations of the Codice Privacy (Italian Privacy Code, D.lgs. 196/2003) as coordinated with the GDPR [N2]. Reference guidelines: EDPB Guidelines 9/2022 on personal data breach notification under the GDPR [E5]; procedure and self-assessment and notification model of the Garante (the Italian DPA) (online breach notification) [G4]; ICO Accountability Framework, "breach response and monitoring" area [S48]. Support and breadth from the checklist corpus: [S1, S2, S9, S10, S11].

**Engagement note**: this area is by construction back-end and organisational. Only J-01 exposes an indicator observable by the scan (the /.well-known/security.txt file and the reporting channel in policy) and J-06 reuses the DPO contact extraction already performed in B-02/H-02; everything else (J-02, J-03, J-04, J-05, J-07, J-08 and the non-observable part of J-01) is verified through documentary review at intake and in the reading of the Art. 28 agreements with processors. The /.well-known/security.txt probe is a passive, non-intrusive HTTP request.

**Client questions (Area J)**

**Detection and response plan**
- How would you become aware of a data breach? Do you have monitoring and alerting on accesses, databases and systems that process personal data (SIEM, log review, anomalous access alerts)? Who receives the alerts and with what availability?
- Is there a written incident response plan? Who is part of it, what is the escalation chain, what are the containment criteria and actions (system isolation, access blocking)? When was it last updated or tested?

**Notification to the Garante and clauses with processors**
- Who decides whether and what to notify to the Garante, within what internal flow, in order to meet the 72 hours of Art. 33? Do you have a notification model already prepared and do you know the Garante's online notification channel?
- In the agreements with data processors (hosting, agencies, SaaS providers, PSP) is a data breach clause provided, with the obligation to report the incident to you without undue delay and within certain timeframes (cf. Art. 28 and Area E)?

**Communication to data subjects and encryption**
- Do you have a documented criterion to establish when a breach entails a "high risk" and therefore must be communicated to data subjects under Art. 34? Do you have a communication template with the protective actions that users can adopt?
- Is personal data at rest encrypted (cf. F-12)? Encryption is the premise for the exemption from communication to data subjects under Art. 34(3)(a).

**Breach register and forensic investigation**
- Do you keep a register of all breaches, including those you decided not to notify, with the justification for the decision (Art. 33(5))? Is it distinct from the Art. 30 record of processing?
- In the event of an incident, which logs do you have available for the forensic investigation and for how long do you retain them (where, when, how, which data, which users involved)? Is there a root cause analysis procedure and a procedure for updating the measures following the incident?

**Governance, history and coverage**
- Do you periodically review the trend of breaches over time (trends, recurrences) and bring the outcomes to company top management so they translate into objectives and corrective actions?
- Have you already suffered breaches or security incidents? How did you handle them: notification to the Garante under Art. 33, communication to data subjects under Art. 34, corrective measures adopted?
- Do you have cyber insurance coverage or an incident response and forensics provider that can be activated in an emergency?
